Mechanical Engineering: Jobs


Photos by ThisisEngineering and Sten Rademaker on Unsplash


Wondering what sorts of jobs you can get as a mechanical engineer? Or what the job prospects are? Or how much you're likely to get paid? Well, then you've come to the right place!

A job in mechanical engineering requires at minimum a Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering or something related such as a Bachelor of Applied Science. Generally, mechanical engineers don't have difficulty finding jobs. According to Job Bank, the job prospects for mechanical engineering in Canada range from moderate to good. Experts predict there may be a shortage of mechanical engineers in Canada between 2024 and 2033, which could lead to higher demand in the field. Please note that this is subject to change.


Photo by Sam Loyd and ThisisEngineering on Unsplash


According to Job Bank, in 2023, the average retirement age was 67 years old. In addition, in 2023, there were 44,900 reported mechanical engineers employed.

According to Indeed, the average salary in Canada is $85,359. On average, mechanical engineers on the entry level make around $58,232 per year. Senior engineers make around $114,197 per year. In addition, they reported that one of the highest paying cities in Canada is Calgary, Alberta, with $103,548 per year, closely followed by Saskatoon, Saskatchewan, with $100,271 per year and Toronto, Ontario, with $97,667 per year. Keep in mind that these numbers are based off a small percentage of the population, and may change. Please note that salaries vary greatly between sources and may not be entirely accurate.


There are many jobs you can go into after becoming a mechanical engineer, such as:

  • Product design and development

  • Energy (oil, gas, and renewable energy)

  • Engines and automotives

  • Heating, ventilation, and air conditioning

  • MicroElectroMechanical Systems

  • Maintenance and reliability

  • Consulting and management

  • Naval architecture

  • Aerospace

  • Pulp and paper

  • Manufacturing

  • Robotics

  • Biomedical

  • Forest products

  • Machinery design

Photos by ThisisEngineering on Unsplash


These are all things that have to do with designing, developing, and maintaining mechanical systems. That's what mechanical engineering is about - working with machines and figuring out how to build them, test them, and fix them. The amount of hands on work versus desk work will depend on the job, though in general, mechanical engineers do more designing and testing then actual building or repairs. It can be a challenging job, but it can also be very rewarding.
