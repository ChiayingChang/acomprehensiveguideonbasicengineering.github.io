Chemical Engineering: Jobs


Photo by Diane Serik and Jaron Nix on Unsplash


Wondering what sorts of jobs you can get as a chemical engineer? Or what the job prospects are? Or how much you're likely to get paid? Well then you've come to the right place!

A job in chemical engineering requires at minimum a Bachelor's degree in Chemical Engineering or something related such as a Bachelor of Applied Science. Generally, chemical engineers don't have difficulty finding jobs. According to Job Bank, the job prospects for chemical engineering in Canada range from moderate to good. Experts predict that the job prospects will remain roughly as they are now from 2024-2033. Please note that this is subject to change.


Photo by National Cancer Institute on Unsplash


According to Indeed, the average salary in Canada is $79,131 pery year, while according to Payscale, it's $70,519 per year. In addition, Indeed states that chemical engineers on the junior level make around $69,822 per year, while senior engineers make around $126,732 per year. They also reported that one of the highest paying cities in Canada is Vancouver, BC, with $102,266 per year, closely followed by Maple Ridge, BC, with $93,369 per year, and Calgary, Alberta with $92,880 per year. Keep in mind that these numbers may change depending on how many people report their salaries on the website, and are only based off a small percentage of the population. Please note that salaries vary greatly between sources and may not be entirely accurate.


There are many jobs you can go into after becoming a chemical engineer, such as:

  • Chemical technology

  • Chemical plant operatoring

  • Analytical chemistry

  • Food engineering

  • Pharmaceutical manufacturering

  • Environmental engineering

  • Energy engineering

  • Food fortification

  • Consulting engineering

  • Operation technical support

  • Design engineering

  • Biotechnology

  • Processing engineering

  • Cooling process development

  • Environmental waste management

  • Food science

Photo by ThisisEngineering on Unsplash


These are all things that have to do with designing, developing, and working with chemicals. The amount of hands on work versus desk work will depend on the job. Chemical engineers spend the majority of their time in an office or a lab.
