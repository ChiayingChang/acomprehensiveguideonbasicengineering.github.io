Biomedical Engineering: Jobs


Photos by ThisisEngineering on Unsplash


Wondering what sorts of jobs you can get as a biomedical engineer? Or what the job prospects are? Or how much you're likely to get paid? Well then you've come to the right place!

A job in biomedical engineering requires at minimum a Bachelor's degree in Biomedical Engineering or something related such as a Bachelor of Applied Science. Generally, biomedical engineers don't have difficulty finding jobs. According to Job Bank, the job prospects for biomedical engineering in Canada range from moderate to good. Experts predict that the job prospects will remain roughly as they are now from 2024-2033. Please note that this is subject to change.


Photo by ThisisEngineering on Unsplash


According to Indeed, the average salary in Canada is $79,721. In addition, they reported that one of the highest paying cities in Canada is North Battleford, Saskatchewan, with $100,343 per year, closely followed by Toronto, Ontario, with 93,727 per year, and Saskatoon, Saskatchewan, with $93,175 per year. According to Career Explore, biomedical engineers in the US make an average of $84,556 CAD per year. Keep in mind that these numbers may change depending on how many people report their salaries on the website, and are only based off a small percentage of the population. Please note that salaries vary greatly between sources and may not be entirely accurate.


There are many jobs you can go into after becoming a biomedical engineer, such as:

  • Production engineering

  • Research and development engineering

  • Regulatory affairs

  • Biomedical equipment technology

  • Healthcare consultanting

  • Medical sales representing

  • Medical device sales engineering

  • Computational modeling and simulation

  • Biomaterials developing

  • Pharmaceutical engineering

  • Independent consulting

  • Rehabilitation engineering

  • Medical technology developing

  • Biomedical science

  • Teaching

  • Bioprocess engineering

Photo by CDC on Unsplash


These are all things that combine medical sciences with engineering, because that's what biomedical engineering is about; using engineering knowledge to create medical devices to address healthcare problems. Biomedical engineers work in places like offices, clinics, and labs. In offices, they often are analyzing data and designing different products. In a clinic, they may work together with other healthcare professionals. And in labs, they experiment with different things and test designs of different prototypes. They may even work in manufacturing settings, where they provide expertise in quality control, production, and hygiene standards.
