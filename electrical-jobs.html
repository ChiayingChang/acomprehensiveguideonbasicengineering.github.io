Electrical Engineering: Jobs


Photos by ThisisEngineering on Unsplash


Wondering what sorts of jobs you can get as an electrical engineer? Or what the job prospects are? Or how much you're likely to get paid? Well then you've come to the right place!

A job in electrical engineering requires at minimum a Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering or something related such as a Bachelor of Applied Science. Generally, electrical engineers don't have difficulty finding jobs. According to Job Bank, the job prospects for mechanical engineering in Canada range from moderate to good. Experts predict there may be a shortage of mechanical engineers in Canada between 2024 and 2033, which could lead to higher demand in the field. Please note that this is subject to change.


Photo by Nicolas Thomas and Artiom Vallat on Unsplash


According to Indeed, the average salary in Canada is $90,895. On average, electrical engineers on the entry level make around $30.62 per hour, while senior engineers make around $114,598 per year. In addition, they reported that one of the highest paying cities in Canada was Mississauga, Ontario, with $113,586 per year, closely followed by Calgary, Alberta, with $99,916 per year and Vancouver, BC, with $99,098 per year. Keep in mind that these numbers are based off a small percentage of the population, and may change. Please note that salaries vary greatly between sources and may not be entirely accurate.


There are many jobs you can go into after becoming a electrical engineer, such as:

  • Special effects technology

  • Sound engineering

  • Technical writing

  • Acoustic consultanting

  • Design engineering

  • Controls engineering

  • Aerospace engineer

  • Communications engineering

  • Instrumentation engineering

  • Power engineering

  • Computer engineering

  • Radio frequency engineering

  • Broadcast engineering

  • Circuit designing

  • Energy efficiency engineering

  • Hardware engineering

Photo by Jeswin Thomas on Unsplash


These are all things that have to do with things like electronic devices and technology, because electrical engineering is all about designing, building, and testing electrical products. The amount of hands on work versus desk work will depend on the job, though electrical engineers tend to spend more time designing and testing things.
