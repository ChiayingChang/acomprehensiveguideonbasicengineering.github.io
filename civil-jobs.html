Civil Engineering: Jobs


Photo by Scott Blake and Peter Marques on Unsplash


Wondering what sorts of jobs you can get as a civil engineer? Or what the job prospects are? Or how much you're likely to get paid? Well then you've come to the right place!

A job in civil engineering requires at minimum a Bachelor's degree in Civil Engineering or something related such as a Bachelor of Applied Science. Generally, civil engineers don't have difficulty finding jobs. According to Job Bank, the job prospects for civil engineering in Canada range from moderate to good. Experts predict there may be a shortage of mechanical engineers in Canada between 2024 and 2033, which could lead to higher demand in the field. Please note that this is subject to change.


Photo by Nathan Waters and Glenov Brankovic on Unsplash


According to Indeed, the average salary in Canada is $90,685. They reported that one of the highest paying cities in Canada is Toronto, Ontario, with $108,396 per year, closely followed by Ottawa, Ontario, with $107,527 per year and Burnaby, BC, with $103,683 per year. According to another website, the salary of civil engineers in the US range from $42,200 per year for entry level engineers to $178,640 per year, with an average salary of $106,314. Keep in mind that these numbers may change depending on how many people report their salaries on the website, and are only based off a small percentage of the population. Please note that salaries vary greatly between sources and may not be entirely accurate.


There are many jobs you can go into after becoming a civil engineer, such as:

  • Surveying

  • Building engineering

  • Water hygiene engineering

  • Urban plannering

  • Field service engineering

  • Construction engineering

  • Environmental engineering

  • Construction estimating

  • Geotechnical engineering

  • Water resources engineering

  • Consulting engineering

  • Planning engineering

  • Engineering project managing

  • City engineering

  • Fire engineering

  • Transport planning

Photo by John Kakuk on Unsplash


Depending on the job, there are a variety of different locations that you may be working on, such as at a desk, on field locations, or at construction sites. In the office, civil engineers spend a large portion of their time on computers and work with other professionals such as architects to complete their projects. On field locations, they check the environment around them for safety and complete things like quality checks and and inspections. And on the construction site, they make sure that everything is being carried out safely according to plan and deal with problems as they come up.
